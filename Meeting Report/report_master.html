Methodology and important information

Notes for interpreting this report.

Purpose of this document

This pre-meeting review document is prepared to support a planning conversation. It highlights factual information, planning assumptions, and agenda items that should be confirmed before recommendations are finalised.

  • It is not intended to be a complete record of advice or a standalone recommendation.
  • Any agreed changes should be confirmed after the meeting before implementation.

Projection methodology

Retirement projections are illustrative and are based on the inputs, assumptions, and capital events recorded at the date prepared.

  • Projected income is shown in today’s money and before tax unless stated otherwise.
  • Actual outcomes may differ because of investment returns, inflation, fees, tax, legislation, contribution changes, and personal circumstances.

Investment and account information

Account values, asset splits, contribution levels, growth-asset exposure, and offshore exposure are planning inputs used to guide the discussion.

  • Values should be checked against the most recent available investment statements.
  • Portfolio changes should only be implemented after the agreed advice and instruction process.

Risk and estate information

Policy and estate summaries are factual planning summaries and should be read together with the official policy schedules, beneficiary nominations, wills, and estate-planning documents.

  • Benefit amounts, beneficiaries, ownership, waiting periods, executors, guardians, and trustees should be confirmed where relevant.
  • Estate, tax, and legal matters may require input from appropriately qualified professionals.

Regulatory and confidentiality note

It should not be relied on by any other person. Information in this report depends on the accuracy of data provided by the client, product providers, and internal planning records.
