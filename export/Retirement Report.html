X.How this projection was built

Methodology, in plain English.

How the capital grows

Each month, contributions from both spouses are added to their retirement and discretionary pools. The combined balance compounds at the expected nominal return, applied monthly.

Contributions escalate annually at the assumed rate, mirroring how payroll-linked savings typically keep pace with salary growth.

From future rands to today's money

All headline figures — the monthly income, the capital at retirement — are shown in today's purchasing power. We deflate the nominal projection by CPI for each year of the horizon.

Where future (nominal) rands appear, they are labelled explicitly.

The retirement income figure

The monthly-income result applies a 5% annual draw-down to the real capital at retirement, divided by twelve. It is a planning-level guide to sustainable income, not a guaranteed payout.

The three-part breakdown

We decompose the final capital into three components:

  • Starting capital, compounded. Today's combined balances left to grow for the full horizon.
  • Cumulative contributions. Every rand paid in, summed across the horizon.
  • Growth on contributions. The compounding return earned on those contributions, calculated as the residual once the first two components are subtracted from the total.

Capital events

No one-off inflows or outflows are modelled in this projection.

What this projection is not

It is not tax advice, not a forecast, and not a guarantee. Investment returns are uncertain and may differ materially from the assumptions used here.

Risk & market assumptions

The expected return, inflation, and escalation figures used here are long-term planning assumptions for a diversified growth-oriented portfolio. Actual returns are volatile; sequence-of-returns risk is not modelled. The 5% draw-down rate is a widely-used planning heuristic, not a guaranteed sustainable rate.

Tax treatment

Figures are shown before tax. Retirement-fund contributions, investment growth within retirement funds, and income drawn in retirement are each subject to different tax rules. Your personal tax position may change the effective outcome materially.

Review cadence

We recommend reviewing this projection at least annually, or whenever household circumstances, contribution rates, or market conditions shift meaningfully.
